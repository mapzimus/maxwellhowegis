2

Write the Pitch

~15 minutes · solo · center spread, pencil

A pitch is one paragraph that names four things. That's it. That's the whole move.

WHO your person, plus one solid fact about them
HOW your format, plus its shape: how many letters, panels, stops, verses
WHAT FROM 3 or 4 pages from YOUR Journal, named by day. Your folder is the evidence locker.
WHY ME the honest reason this voice pulls you

Stuck starting? Steal these:

  1. "My person is ___. He/she ___."
  2. "My format is ___: ___ (four letters, six panels, eight stops)."
  3. "My evidence is my Day ___, Day ___, and Day ___ pages."
  4. "I picked this person because ___."
FOLDER FINDER, where your evidence lives: Day 4 triangle map · Day 5 crossing page · Day 6 museum notes · Day 7 choice writing · Day 8 push and pull chart · Day 9 your Articles · Day 10 storm page and wreck map · Day 11 harbor sketch map · Day 12 grid archaeology · Day 13 chokepoint pages · Day 14 then and now trace. Plus every Captain's Log entry you have written.
LOG IT: The paragraph. 4 to 6 sentences, all four names in it, pencil. Flip your folder and touch each source page you claim. If you can't touch it, don't claim it.
3

The Sharpening Round

~12 minutes · pairs · pitcher and whetstone, then swap

A blade gets sharp against a stone. Your partner is the stone.

  1. Pitch out loud. One minute. Partner times it.
  2. Partner asks one sharpening question. Not a compliment. A question that finds the soft spot.
  3. Write the question on your spread. Word for word.
  4. Swap roles. Do it again.
  5. Both of you: revise. Answer the question inside your paragraph.

Questions that sharpen: "Which Journal page proves that?" · "Can you really build six panels by Wednesday?" · "Why this person and not your other pick?" · "What does a visitor see first at your table?" Questions that don't: "Is it good?" · "I like it."

LEAGUE POINTS, flipped: today the points go to the ASKERS, not the pitchers. Best sharpening question of the day: 5. Second best: 2. Max is listening while he circulates. Help hard.
4

The Captain's Check

runs all block · 2 minutes when your turn comes · bring your sheet AND your folder

Every pitch gets checked today. Every single one. Here's how the queue works:

  1. Pitch revised? Add your name to the queue on the board.
  2. Keep working while you wait. Tighten the paragraph. Pull your source pages to the front of your folder so you can grab them fast.
  3. Your turn: 2 minutes with Max. He checks four things.
Person real? a person from our record, one solid fact ready
Format buildable? finishable by Wednesday with our gear
Sources named? 3 or 4 Journal pages, by day, sitting in your folder
Why you? you can say why this voice pulls you

Hear "not yet"? Not yet is not no. You get one named fix. Make it, re-queue, done. Pass, and your PITCH page gets the captain's mark. You are cleared for the build.

Cleared early? Write your build list on the spread: three materials you'll want Tuesday morning. Then game time: Navigator or Black Sam.
