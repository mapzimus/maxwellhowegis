One Move: Give Them a Voice

15 minutes · with your teacher · then back to work

Your person was real. You were not there. So how do you write their words without putting words in their mouth? One move. Every invented line stands on a documented fact.

  1. Write the fact. Note where it comes from.
  2. Ask: what would my person say about this?
  3. Write one line in their voice that stands on that fact.

THE FACT, from the record: 9 November 1716. Pirates take the sloop Bonetta. A witness wrote down what happened. A boy named John King, eight to eleven years old, demanded to join them. His mother could not stop him.

A line that stands on it: "My mother held my arm. I chose the pirates anyway."

A line that floats free: "I always dreamed of being famous." The record says nothing about his dreams. Cut it, or find a fact it can stand on.

The test: point at the fact under your line. No fact under it? It floats. Fix it.

LOG IT: Pick one fact from your own Journal. Write one line in your person's voice that stands on it. That line goes into your project today.
