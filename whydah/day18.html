The Review

~35 minutes · pairs your teacher posts · both partners get both turns

Mapmakers never shipped a chart alone. A second navigator checked it first. Today you are each other's second navigator.

Your partner is not your best friend. On purpose. Your partner builds in a different format than you. Fresh eyes catch what yours skip.

Step 1: SHOW maker walks the partner through the work, like they are a visitor tomorrow. Two minutes. Then quiet.
Step 2: "I noticed..." reviewer names one exact thing that already works. Point at it.
Step 3: "I wonder..." ONE question a visitor might ask. Just one.
Step 4: "What if..." ONE change that would make the work stronger. Just one.
Step 5: WRITE + THANKS maker writes the question and the suggestion, word for word. Then says thank you.
Step 6: SWAP trade roles. Same steps, other direction.
REVIEWER'S CODE: one question, one suggestion. Not five. Choosing is the skill. Talk about the work, not the person. Makers: no defending. Write it down. You rule on it in the next block, not now.
LOG IT: THE REVIEW page holds your partner's "I wonder..." and "What if...", word for word. Their name signed under it. A real survey gets signed.
2

Fix Their Thing First

~35 minutes · solo, performers with their cast · Chromebook only if your format needs one

You hold a real note from a real reviewer. Captains act on the survey before they sail. One rule runs this whole block:

THE PRIORITY RULE: fix the thing your partner named FIRST. Not the thing you feel like fixing. Feelings pick the easy fix. Your reviewer pointed at the real one.
  1. MY REVISION PLAN page. Line 1 is your partner's "What if..." Add two or three more fixes under it, biggest first.
  2. Work top to bottom. Cross each line off. A crossed line is proof you moved.
  3. Performers: your fix is rehearsal. Two full runs, start to finish, at real volume. No stopping mid-run. Fix between runs.
  4. Want the captain's eyes on something? Put your name on the CAPTAIN'S TABLE list on the board. Keep working. I come to you.
What finished looks like →
LOG IT: line 1 crossed off before anything else. Whole list done early? Add one polish line. Bigger title. Cleaner edges. Smoother words.

Fast finishers: walk your own station like a stranger. Stand where the visitor will stand. Say your opener out loud. Does the first glance tell them who and what?

Set the Stage

10 minutes · every hand · crew assignments on the board

Tomorrow's room does not build itself. Ten minutes. Move like a crew docking a ship.

STATION CREW tables into stations, one per project, walking room between them
CARD CREW a table card at every station: project title, your name, your person's name. Readable from three steps away.
JOURNAL CREW one table by the door for the Voyage Journals, a few opened to strong pages

Last move, everyone: set your own station. Work displayed. Card placed. Chair out of the walkway.

Leave it ready tonight. Tomorrow morning you straighten. You do not build.
