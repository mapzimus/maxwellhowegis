The land brief: four ships, one choice

About 10 minutes · on land, before vests · just listen

It is 1717. You're broke, and the sea is where the work is. Four doors are open to a sailor. What's behind each one?

MERCHANT SHIP haul cargo for an owner's profit. Rotten food, short pay. The worst runs were the slave voyages: about 1 in 5 of the crew died on those.
ROYAL NAVY the king's warships. Pay was set in 1653 and never raised. Press gangs grabbed men off the street and forced them aboard.
FISHING BOAT cold, wet, hard, poor. But most nights you sleep at home.
PIRATE CREW you vote on the big calls. The loot splits into written shares. And if the law catches you, you hang.

Three doors push. One pulls. That is the engine of our whole question: why would a person choose piracy in 1717?

Rank the four in your head, best life to worst. Hold your answer. The water gets a vote today.

The water mission: dead reckoning

On the water · no tools · that's the point

Sailors had no GPS. None. They knew three things: speed, direction, time. From those three they worked out where they were. That is dead reckoning. Men bet their lives on it.

On the water today, try it:

  1. Pick a landmark. A buoy, a dock, a tree on the far shore.
  2. Guess your distance. Say it out loud. Out loud takes nerve.
  3. Check yourself. Paddle it and count your strokes. How close were you?

Then read the current. Stop paddling for ten seconds. Which way does the water want you to go? Sailors could feel this in the dark. Now you feel it too.

THE RECKONING: once we're out there, your captain points at one landmark. Every sailor calls one distance. Closest call wins 5 league points, second closest takes 2. One call each. No takebacks.
4

The law of the water

Non-negotiable · launch to landing

Plenty of 1717 sailors never learned to swim. The sea was their workplace, and it could kill them. You get two things they never had: a life-vest and a professional guide. Take both seriously.

  1. On the water, the outfitter's word is law. Not advice. Law. They know this water. You don't.
  2. Life-vest on, clipped, the whole time. Never off. Not for a second.
  3. Buddy pairs. You see your buddy at all times. When a check is called, find your buddy fast.
  4. Not sure about something? Ask. Real sailors ask. Guessing is for landmarks, nothing else.
