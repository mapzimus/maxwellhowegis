Orthodromes & Antipodal Geometry

What if any city on Earth were the North Pole? Every point on a sphere has a unique "personal equator" — the orthodrome (great circle) perpendicular to the axis through that point and its antipode. This is a research-and-explainer companion to Geopuesto: it walks through spherical geometry from first principles, traces the concept's long history in cartography and astronomy (formally: the antipodal equatorial orthodrome, used since Ptolemy's "oblique sphere" and behind every oblique Mercator projection), and lets you draw any city's personal equator on a 3D globe. Geopuesto explores what's at the antipode; this explores the geometry behind it.
