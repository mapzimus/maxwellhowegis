// About

Who I Am

I'm Maxwell Howe — a GIS analyst and cartographer with a Master's degree in Geo-Information Science from Salem State University (Class of 2025). I build maps and spatial analyses that make complex geographic data clear, compelling, and actionable.

My graduate work spanned the full GIS stack: remote sensing and digital image processing with Landsat and Sentinel imagery, spatial database design with PostGIS, computer cartography, advanced quantitative methods, and Python-based geoprocessing automation. I've applied these skills to real-world projects for municipal governments, non-profit organizations, and academic research.

Outside of GIS, I teach Probability and Statistics at Lynn English High School, where I bring data literacy and spatial thinking into the classroom every day. I also created Mapzimus — a scoring and correlation engine for systematically identifying the most compelling story angles in spatial data for viral infographic maps.

I'm currently looking for my next role in GIS, remote sensing, or spatial data science. If you have spatial data that needs a voice, let's talk.
