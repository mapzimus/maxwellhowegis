About

I'm Maxwell Howe — a GIS professional and builder with an M.S. in Geo-Information Science from Salem State University. I combine spatial analysis, R and Python scripting, and web development to build tools that make geographic data useful and accessible.

I teach math and statistics at the high school level, and that shapes how I approach GIS work: most of the job is translating complex spatial stories into something a non-technical audience can actually act on. I know how to bridge that gap. It's why I build tools people can use — and why cartographic communication matters to me as much as technically correct output.

Post-graduation, I've kept building independently: TappyMaps, Howe2Math, a shelf of client-side web tools, and a steady stream of interactive maps — from a statewide education atlas to a LiDAR survey of drowned villages.

I'm targeting GIS, data science, and adjacent technical roles — hybrid or remote in MA/NH/RI. Open to GIS analyst, spatial data scientist, and GIS developer positions, plus freelance mapping work.
