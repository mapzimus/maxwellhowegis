Data comes from MA DESE, the U.S. Census / ACS, CDC PLACES, and MassGIS. Full credits live under “Sources” at the bottom of the panel. For the full statewide picture, see the MA Education Atlas.

Census tract
A small Census-defined neighborhood area (Lynn has 22). Tract metrics here are Census/ACS & health figures, available for Lynn only.
Choropleth
A map that shades each area by a value — darker (or more intense) color means a higher number.
Bivariate
A choropleth showing two metrics at once on a 3×3 color grid, so you can see where both are high, both low, or mixed.
Jenks / Quantile classification
Ways of grouping values into color classes. Jenks (natural breaks) finds natural gaps; Quantile puts an equal count of areas in each class.
MCAS
Massachusetts' statewide student test (grades 3–8 and 10). “% M+E” is the share Meeting or Exceeding expectations.
Per-pupil $
Dollars spent per student, per year.
EL (English Learner)
Students still learning English.
Low Income
Students from economically disadvantaged households (DESE definition).
SWD (Students with Disabilities)
Students with an active Individualized Education Program (IEP).
Dropout vs. attrition
Dropout = students who leave school without graduating. Attrition = any student who leaves a school (transfers, moves), a broader count.
CDC PLACES health metrics
Model-based estimates of adult health (obesity, diabetes, asthma, etc.) at the tract level, expressed as a percent of adults.
ACS
The Census Bureau's American Community Survey — the source of the tract income, language, education, and housing figures.
FTE
Full-Time Equivalent — a way of counting staff so part-timers add up to whole positions.
